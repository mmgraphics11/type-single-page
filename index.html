Bricolage Grotesque

History

The font Bricolage Grotesque is a well known open source san serif typeface which was released in the early 2020s. It was developed by The League of Moveable Type, and designed by Thomas Gillett in 2023.The name of the font itself has its own meaning, "Bricolage" is French for "do-it-yourself" or "construction from varied materials". While Grostesque is the style and aesthetic of the font. The font itself has three axis weight, width, and optical size which allows designers to have precise control over the fonts appearance Stylistically, Bricolage Grotesque plays with traditional grotesque proportions but introduces gentle curves, and unexpected contrasts. These features give it more "personality" than other minimalist geometric or neo-grotesque fonts.
